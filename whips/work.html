Home Different Types How Bullwhips Work

Whips have a simple funcation of making a very loud noise. The whip spins fast and then makes a crack. The crack can even break the sound barrier

When you crack a bullwhip, the tip (called the popper) travels so fast it breaks the sound barrier, creating a tiny sonic boom—that's the loud crack you hear. That whip tip can hit speeds over 1,400 feet per second (about 970 mph). It’s like holding a supersonic jet in your hand… made of leather.
