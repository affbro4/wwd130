Respond: Incident Response (VM environment))

  • Due Feb 28 at 11:59pm
  • Points 20
  • Questions 5
  • Time Limit None
  • Allowed Attempts Unlimited

Instructions

Introduction

NIST defines the Respond function as "Develop and implement appropriate activities to take action regarding a detected cybersecurity incident.” The Respond Function supports the ability to contain the impact of a potential cybersecurity incident.

You will work together to respond to an attack that has happened in our network.

As you do, keep the Incident Response Life Cycle in mind.  Try to follow the steps in the life cycle.

 

Getting Started:

  • Do NOT do this assignment ahead of time.  We will work on this in class.  Anyone that misses this class period will need to do this on their own.
  • When you log into your systems, it will be very evident that someone has messed with your system.

Part 1: Prepare to Make a Plan

  • Based on what you've briefly learned from logging in, you realize you have a problem.  You should stop what you're doing and refer to your Incident Response Plan.  There's a chance that you don't have an Incident Response Plan, so here's your chance. 
  • Come up with a simple plan for what to do.  This could involve things like blocking suspicious activity, changing passwords, or updating security settings.  This also would include things like, who do you notify?  Do you take the infected machines off the network?  
  • Write down your plan in a clear and easy-to-understand way. Think about what steps need to be taken right away and what can wait.

Part 2: Detect & Analyze

  • Start to investigate and try to determine what happened.  See if what you see are true-positives or false-positives.

Part 3: Contain, Eradicate, & Recover

  • Your work now needs to turn to limit the damage, get the attackers out of the network, and then return to business as normal.

Part 4: Post-Incident Activity

  • You've gone through an incident.  However, the work is not over.  It's only a matter of time before another attacker comes at you again.  Go back through the things that you've done and learned from this experience.  Think about the things that you can incorporate into the next iteration of your Incident Response Plan and make sure you get that updated.
